Team contributions

Adam Pielbusch
E-mail | LinkedIn
  • Creating the initial markers. The first iteration of the game consisted of QR-codes that was used as markers where I filled the Vuforia database and made the different markers generate the correct game object.
  • User interface All the buttons and most of their functionality as well as scaleability (landscape/portrait mode). This includes the attach function as well as the reset and restart buttons and the help text on the top.
  • User experience The UI was tested on a small group of people (mostly family) to see they were visible enough as well as located in a way that they were easily reached but still not in the way of the gameplay.
  • Camera man for the demo video.
Elias Lousseief
E-mail | LinkedIn
  • 3D Modelling doing the first base part of the final map in Maya.
  • Actor in the Knight Rider demo video.
  • Artist creating the poster for Mission IX.
  • Experimenter doing an intermediate set of fiducials which were later abandoned as well as trying out large fiducials and exploring the use of 3D fiducials.
  • Programmer doing a part of the script attaching markers to the screen.
Erik Lindbom
E-mail | LinkedIn
  • 3D Modelling in Maya doing the fire ring, cactuses, trees, bridge, car wreck and stones including all textures. Also modelled parts of map such as the water, including textures on the map.
  • Special effects such as particle systems for the fire on the ring (including fire, sparks and smoke) and explosion of the car. Added sound effects to the explosion. Also made the phone vibrate when an explosion occurs.
  • Gameplay scripting including early stage generating a small road under the car in the same direction as the car, measuring the distance between the car and the ramp to make the road end by the ramp (all other areas makes the car explode). Script for when the car is exploding, a car wreck is replacing the car under the flames and falls to the ground. Script for when jumping through the ring, fire expands.
  • AR setting up Vuforia with first markers for first demo.
  • Actor in the Knight Rider demo video.
  • Level design
Lucas Garnot
E-mail | LinkedIn
  • 3D modeler: Model the car and the board with maya. Do the rendering and the texturing. I used a particle system to model exhausted fumes in Unity.
  • Artist: Making the icon for the game and the first poster
  • Gameplay scripting: I helped to write the script to dynamically adjust and update the height of the objects on the field.
Tobias Hindersson
E-mail | LinkedIn
  • Creating the final markers/fiducials for the game objects and for the game board. Along with creating and animating the sprites for the turning and booster objects that appear in the game after placing the corresponding markers.
  • Gameplay scripting, implementing the game logic that makes the car accelerate, jump, turn and boost and such, along with tweaking the physics and collision handling. Was also responsible for the script that dynamically adjusts the in-game height of markers when placed, to always be positioned at the right height in relation to the board/level.
  • AR scripting, helping to write the script that attaches the markers to the board and also writing the script that shows a pause panel when no markers are detected. Wrote a script for the original demo that made it possible for markers to attach to one-another when they lost tracking, thereby remaining visible/active.
  • Adding sound effects.
  • Webmaster, making this website.
  • Movie editor, editing our cool video.
